PRIVACY POLICY

This Privacy Policy describes how FerriPay and our affiliates (collectively, “FerriPay,” “we,” and “us”) collect, use, disclose, transfer, store, retain or otherwise process your information when you (“you,” “your”) download the FerriPay app through a relevant app store and sign up for a FerriPay app account and other services through the app or online at  https://ferripay.web.app/ (collectively, “Services”).

Your privacy and trust are important to us. If you use the Services, please also review our Privacy Notice carefully.

This Privacy Policy applies to your use of our Services, and covers only information collected in connection with your access to and use of our Services. Please read this Privacy Policy carefully. By continuing to interact with our Services, you are consenting to the practices described in this Privacy Policy.


Information We Collect About You

We need to collect information about you to provide you with the Services requested. Specifically, we collect (and/or have collected during at least the 12-month period preceding the effective date of this Privacy Policy) the categories of personal information described below. Additionally, you can choose to voluntarily provide information to us.


Information You Provide To Access Our Services

We collect information you provide when you apply or sign up for a FerriPay app account, go through our identity or account verification process, authenticate your account, communicate with us, answer our surveys, participate in contests or promotions offered by us or our partners, or otherwise use our Services.

We collect the following categories of information about you when you use our Services, including:

  1. Personal Data - Demographic and other personally identifiable information, such as a Name, email address, self portrait and government identification in the form of a national identification number, passport or some other government issued identification.

  2. Derivative, login Data - Data relating to your login credentials, such as your IP address, actions made on the FerriPay platform, mobile device information (if accessing FerriPay through a mobile device), including mobile device ID, model, OS, phone number and location.

  3. Financial, transactional Data - Such as your bank account and payment methods you add to your FerriPay app account. This also includes:

    1. Contact information we would ask you on the intended recipients of payments you request us to make. This contact information can be entered manually by providing the contact number or email address of the intended recipient, or by sharing your phone’s contacts with the FerriPay app. You can toggle your contact sharing settings at any time within the FerriPay app.

    2. Transactional information, such as names of transacting parties, the transaction date, amount of funds transacted, a description of the transfer and the device and payment methods used to complete the transaction.

  4. Identification Information - Such as: name, email address, physical address and authentication credentials (for example, information you use to log in to your FerriPay account)

  5. Additional Identification Information for Verified Users - Such as: full mailing address; date of birth; government-issued identification, including Social Security number, passport, or driver’s license.

  6. Financial Information - Such as: bank account and payment card numbers you add to your FerriPay app account.

  7. Contact Information - In order to provide you with the Service, we will also need to collect information from you about the intended recipient of the payment you request us to make. We will therefore ask you to provide contact details of your intended recipient. You can do this by: (a) manually entering a phone number and/or email address into the FerriPay app; or (b) as described below (under Information You Provide to Enhance Your Experience), you can choose to give us access to your phone contacts to facilitate the entry of recipient information. We may also ask you to provide limited access to your phone contacts for account verification purposes. You can update your settings to stop sharing your phone contacts with us at any time, although we will always need a recipient’s phone number or email address to send money to them as requested by you.

  8. Transaction Information - When you use our Services to make, accept, or request payments, we collect information such as: when and where the transactions occur, the names of the transacting parties, a description of the transactions, the payment or transfer amounts, and the devices and payment methods used to complete the transactions.

Cookies and Other Similar Automated Technologies

When you interact with our online services, or open emails we send you, we obtain certain information using automated technologies, such as cookies, web server logs, web beacons, and other technologies. A “cookie” is a text file that websites send to a visitor’s computer or other internet-connected device to uniquely identify the visitor’s browser or to store information or settings in the browser. A “web beacon,” also known as an internet tag, pixel tag, or clear GIF, is a tiny graphic image that may be used in our websites or emails.

We use these automated technologies to collect your device information, internet activity information, and inferences as described above. These technologies help us to:

  • Remember your information so you do not have to re-enter it;

  • Track and understand how you use and interact with our online services and emails;

  • Tailor our online services to your preferences;

  • Measure how useful and effective our services and communications are to you; and

  • Otherwise manage and enhance our products and services.


We set some of these automated technologies ourselves, but others are set by third parties who deliver services on our behalf. For example, we may use other companies’ web analytics services (described in the “Third-Party Analytics Services” section), which use automated technologies to help us evaluate how customers use our websites.

Your browser can alert you when cookies are placed on your device, and how you can stop or disable them via your browser settings. Please note, however, that without cookies all of the features of our online services may not work properly. If you use a mobile device, you can manage how your device and browser share certain device data by changing the privacy and security settings on your mobile device. You can learn more about cookies and how to manage your preferences by visiting http://www.allaboutcookies.org .
